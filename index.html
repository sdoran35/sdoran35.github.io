Ardunio and LED Light Strip Sync to music

I recently wanted to have the lights in our apartment sync to the different beats in the music being played. I began by researching prebuilt products that accomplish this task. What I found is that many products cost upwards of $50. Not something I wanted to invest in, as a college student. I then realized I own a few Ardunio UNO's and some sensors. So, I got to work and first figured out the correct pins for the LED light strip. This ends up being three negative pins for the R,G,B and a single +5V pin for power. If I wanted to extend the length, I would need to invest into a better +12V power supply. Afterwards, I looked into how to parse the input from the digital pin of my sound sensor.
